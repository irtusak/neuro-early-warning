Welcome to NeuroWatch

This dashboard monitors neurological symptom reports across Canada and flags unusual clusters that may indicate emerging health threats. It was built in response to the 2021 New Brunswick mystery illness, where cases accumulated for months before patterns were recognized.

Detection Methodology

▶

Three independent detectors analyze symptom reports, then alerts are merged geographically and ranked by a normalized ensemble scorer.

Detector 1: Spatial Scan (Isolation Forest + DBSCAN)

Learns what "normal" reports look like, flags outliers, then clusters suspicious points geographically (within 50 km). Good at finding unusual symptom/severity combinations in unexpected locations.

Detector 2: Poisson Excess-Rate

Divides Canada into 0.5° grid cells, learns baseline daily rates, and flags cells where recent counts significantly exceed expected. Uses Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction at 5% to control false discovery rate across all cells tested simultaneously.

Detector 3: PyTorch Autoencoder

Neural network trained to reconstruct normal reports. High reconstruction error = anomalous. Uses spatial context features (local count, severity, serious symptom fraction) built from baseline data only to prevent data leakage.

Ensemble Scoring

Detector scores are normalized to rank percentiles for comparability. Merged alerts scored by: max normalized score × detector agreement boost × severity boost × symptom risk weight. Confidence assigned as CRITICAL (3+ detectors), HIGH (2+ or severe), MEDIUM (20+ cases), LOW (other).

Quality Controls (Post Health Canada Review)

  • FDR correction (Benjamini-Hochberg at 5%)
  • Detection on recent data only (no data leakage)
  • Coordinates rounded to ~11 km, small-cell suppression (min 5 cases)
  • Audit logging of all pipeline runs
  • Prion-like symptom risk weighting

About the Data

▶

Since real health data is private, this system uses 5,000 synthetic baseline reports spread across 17 Canadian population centers, weighted by population. Each report includes date, GPS coordinate, symptom type (14 neurological categories), severity (1–5), age, and sex.

120 anomalous cases were injected near rural New Brunswick — mimicking the real 2021–2022 mystery illness: high severity, ages 40–75, dominated by memory loss, cognitive decline, tremors, ataxia, and myoclonus.

Environmental and socioeconomic data is synthetic, modeled on Canada's NPRI, CCME WQI, AQHI, and Statistics Canada datasets.
